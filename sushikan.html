Competitive analysis

I evaluated several competitors that, while not direct rivals to Sushi Kan, still affect its revenue and visibility. Enhancing the website experience with intuitive features will help Sushi Kan differentiate itself and attract more visitors.

The majority of features across competitors were similar, but key differences included:

  • Accessibility: Easily Accessible vs. Hardly Accessible
  • User Flow: Multiple Screens vs. Simplified Interaction
  • Design Aesthetic: Bright/Distracting Interface vs. Minimalistic Interface
  • Product Focus: General Selection vs. Specialization of Products

Design

Wireframes

The wireframes for the Sushi Kan website were simple and clear layouts. They helped plan where key elements like the menu and buttons would be, making sure the website would be easy to navigate and use.

Style

To ensure a consistent visual style, I developed the design system starting with the wordmark, focusing on a clean and welcoming feel. I maintained this style across desktop and mobile screens to keep the experience intuitive and user-friendly. The primary color palette remains white, black, and gray, with the addition of orange for better readability and a stylish touch.

High Fidelity

The high-fidelity designs for Sushi Kan took the basic wireframes and added more detail, like colors, images, and interactive features. These final designs made the website look polished and ensured it matched the restaurant's style.
